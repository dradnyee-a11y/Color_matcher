Color Wheel

How to Use the Color Wheel and Color Palettes

The color wheel is a visual representation of colors arranged according to their chromatic relationship. It is a useful tool for understanding color theory and creating harmonious color combinations.

Complementary Palette

  • Effect: Creates a strong contrast and vibrant look.
  • Use in real life: Great for highlighting important elements, like making a call-to-action button stand out, or combining clothing items for a bold statement.
  • Tip: Use one color as dominant and the other sparingly to avoid visual overload.

Analogous Palette

  • Effect: Produces a harmonious and calming feel because the colors are next to each other on the color wheel.
  • Use in real life: Ideal for outfits, interior design, or any project where you want a smooth, cohesive look.
  • Tip: Choose one color as dominant, the next as secondary, and the third as an accent.

Triadic Palette

  • Effect: Balances vibrancy and harmony. Triadic colors are evenly spaced on the color wheel.
  • Use in real life: Works well for colorful but balanced designs, like playful graphics, websites, or vibrant outfit combinations.
  • Tip: Stick to one dominant color and use the others in smaller amounts to avoid clashing.
